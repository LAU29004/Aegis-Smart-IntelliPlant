INCIDENT REPORT INC-2023-0814
Plant: PLANT-01 | Equipment: HE-01 (Shell and Tube Heat Exchanger, Line 3)
Date of incident: 14 August 2023 | Severity: High | Type: Unplanned outage

DESCRIPTION
Line 3 was shut down for 22 hours due to severe fouling of heat exchanger
HE-01. Process outlet temperature exceeded the high alarm limit and could
not be controlled. On opening, the tube bundle was found heavily fouled
with scale and biological growth on the cooling water side.

PRECURSOR CONDITIONS (from operations log review)
- Ambient temperature remained above 38 degC for six consecutive days
  before the incident.
- Cooling water flow rate had dropped to 82% of design value over the
  preceding week due to a partially blocked inlet strainer.
- Similar precursor conditions were recorded before the fouling events of
  July 2021 (INC-2021-0722) and September 2022 (INC-2022-0918).

ROOT CAUSE
Reduced cooling water velocity during sustained high ambient temperature
accelerated scale deposition and biofouling. Inlet strainer blockage was
the initiating condition.

CORRECTIVE ACTIONS
1. Tube bundle chemically cleaned and hydro-jetted.
2. Cooling water inlet strainer cleaning frequency increased to weekly
   during summer months (April to September).
3. Low-flow alarm set at 88% of design cooling water flow.
4. Biocide dosing program reviewed with water treatment vendor.

OUTCOME
Unplanned downtime of 22 hours (within the 18-24 hour range of the two
previous fouling incidents). No injury. Production loss approximately
180 tonnes.

LESSON LEARNED
When ambient temperature exceeds 38 degC for 5 or more consecutive days
AND cooling water flow falls below 85% of design, HE-01 fouling risk is
high. Inspect the cooling water inlet strainer immediately.
