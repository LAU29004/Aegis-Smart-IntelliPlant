# SOP-OPS-014 — Safe Shutdown Procedure: Boiler B-02

Document type: Standard Operating Procedure | Rev 4 | Approved by: Plant Manager
Applies to: Package Boiler B-02 (8 TPH, 10.5 barg design), Utility Block

## Purpose

Defines the safe, controlled shutdown sequence for Boiler B-02 for planned
maintenance or end-of-campaign stops. For emergency trip conditions refer to
SOP-OPS-015 (Emergency Handling).

## Safe Shutdown Steps

1. Inform the shift supervisor and obtain shutdown clearance. Log the
   shutdown start time in the boiler log book.
2. Gradually reduce boiler load to 30% over not less than 30 minutes to
   avoid thermal shock to the furnace and tubes.
3. Switch fuel firing to low-fire position and stabilise for 10 minutes.
4. Stop the fuel supply: close the fuel gas trip valve and verify the
   burner flame is fully extinguished on the flame scanner.
5. Continue the forced-draft fan in purge mode for a minimum of 5 minutes
   to purge residual combustible gases from the furnace.
6. Stop the FD fan after purge. Close the combustion air damper.
7. Maintain feed water level at normal working level. Do NOT drain the
   boiler while hot; allow natural cooling.
8. When drum pressure falls below 2 barg, open the drum air vent to
   prevent vacuum formation during cool-down.
9. Once the boiler has cooled below 90 °C, the boiler may be drained if
   required for maintenance, using the blowdown valve.
10. Isolate the boiler electrically (LOTO) and tag the fuel, steam, and
    feed water valves closed. Record completion in the log book.

## Safety Notes

- Never leave the boiler unattended between steps 2 and 6.
- Purge (step 5) is mandatory — skipping the purge has caused furnace
  explosions in industry incidents.
- Wear heat-resistant gloves and face shield when operating the air vent
  and blowdown valves.
